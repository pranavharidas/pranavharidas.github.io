Published Work

Study of the Minkowski Function on Smoothly Bounded Quasi-Balanced Pseudoconvex Domains in $\mathbb C^n$

The Minkowski function is a crucial tool in the study of balanced and, more generally, quasi-balanced domains in several complex variables. For a bounded and pseudoconvex quasi-balanced domain, its Minkowski function is known to be plurisubharmonic. This work demonstrates that with the additional assumption of a smooth boundary, the Minkowski function of a quasi-balanced domain is in fact smooth away from the origin. This result facilitates the construction of a smooth plurisubharmonic defining function for such domains and is a novel finding even in the case of balanced domains. This is a joint work with Jaikrishnan Janardhanan (IIT Palakkad) and was published in Complex Variables and Elliptic Equations in 2021. A significant portion of this work was completed at the Chennai Mathematical Institute.
P. Haridas and J. Janardhanan, A note on the smoothness of the Minkowski function, Complex Var. Elliptic Equ. 66 (2021), no.~4, 541--545; MR4224759

Proper Holomorphic Mappings of Quasi-Balanced Domains in $\mathbb C^3$

This paper investigates proper holomorphic self-maps on quasi-balanced domains in $\mathbb C^3$ with specific boundary regularity properties. A long-standing conjecture in Several Complex Variables posits that proper holomorphic self-maps of any smoothly bounded pseudoconvex domain in $\mathbb C^n$ are necessarily automorphisms. This was proven for the unit ball in $\mathbb C^n, n>1$ by Alexander (1977), and extended to strictly pseudoconvex domains by Pinchuk (1980). Subsequent work by Bedford and Bell (1982) and Huang and Pan (1996) further demonstrated this for weakly pseudoconvex domains with real-analytic boundaries.

The primary objective of this paper is to establish Alexander-type theorems for smoothly bounded quasi-balanced domains of finite type in $\mathbb C^3$. Specifically, the paper proves that every proper holomorphic self-mapping of a smoothly-bounded pseudoconvex quasi-balanced domain of (D’Angelo) finite type is an automorphism. This is a joint work with Jaikrishnan Janardhanan and was published in Complex Analysis and its Synergies in 2023.
P. Haridas and J. Janardhanan, Proper holomorphic mappings of quasi-balanced domains in $\mathbb{C}^3$, Complex Anal. Synerg. 9 (2023), no.~2, Paper No. 7, 7 pp.; MR4558996

Ongoing Work

Surface Braid Groups and their Representations

The study of Braid groups, initiated by Artin in 1925, remains a classical and active area of research due to their crucial role in knot theory, link theory, and 3-manifolds. The study of their representations also continues to be an active area, with connections to quantum groups and knot invariants. Surface braid groups, defined as the fundamental group of the configuration space over an arbitrary compact surface, are an equally interesting object of study. While Curtis McMullen (2012) explored unitary representations of the braid group via Hodge theory of cyclic branched coverings of $\mathbb P^1$, this ongoing work investigates the geometric representation of the surface braid group. The manuscript is nearing completion. This is a joint work with Saswathi Mukherjee and Chitrabhanu Chaudhuri (NISER Bhubaneswar).

Liftability of Braids and Mapping Class Groups

This work, in collaboration with my PhD scholar Ms. Athira E. V., focuses on the study of liftability of braids and, more generally, of the mapping class group in the case of general cyclic branched covers over $\mathbb{P}^1$. This research extends existing work by Tyrone Ghaswala and Winarski on specific covers over $\mathbb{P}^1$, aiming to identify and characterize the liftable mapping class groups in this broader context. Additionally, a study of the geometric representations of the pure braid groups is planned as an integral part of this project.

MSc Project/MSc Colloquium Reading Course Supervision

The following MSc students have completed projects or colloquium reading courses under my supervision. They are listed in reverse chronological order of completion.

Shafil Maheen N (Ongoing MSc Project, KSoM)

Shafil Maheen N's MSc thesis studies Royden's theorem, a foundational result in Teichmüller theory. This theorem states that the Teichmüller metric and the Kobayashi metric on Teichmüller space are identical. This means that both metrics, used to measure distances in Teichmüller space, yield equivalent results, which is a significant aspect of complex analysis and geometry.

Sreelakshmi M (Ongoing MSc Project, Kannur University)

Sreelakshmi M's project focuses on the Corona Theorem, specifically T. Wolff's elementary proof. This work investigates the density of the open unit disc in the maximal ideal space of the Banach algebra of bounded analytic functions. The proof, unlike earlier versions, does not rely on Carleson measures and BMO, and integrates elements of $H^p$-theory (including inner-outer factorization), properties of $\overline{\partial}$-operators, and Green's formula for the unit disc.

Anagha U S (MSc Project, Amrita Viswa Vidyapeetham)

This project explored the torus and its meromorphic functions, which reduce to doubly periodic meromorphic functions (elliptic functions) on the complex plane. By studying elliptic functions, the behavior of meromorphic functions on the torus was elucidated. The dissertation also delved into the Weierstrass $\wp$-function, a primary example of an elliptic function with two poles, and its properties, including how any elliptic function can be expressed as a rational function of $\wp$ and its derivative. Anagha has successfully transitioned to a role at IBM India in Bangalore, applying her analytical skills developed during her project.

Muhammed Faheem (MSc Colloquium Reading Course)

This course focused on de Branges' Proof of the Bieberbach Conjecture. Schlicht functions are univalent functions $f:\mathbb{D}(0,1) \rightarrow \mathbb{C}$ normalized such that $f(0)=0$ and $f'(0)=1$. The Bieberbach conjecture, proven by Louis de Branges in 1985, states that if $f(z)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n z^n$ is a Schlicht function, then $|a_n|\leq n$. Faheem's study included the historical context, such as Bieberbach's proof for $|a_2|\leq 2$ (1916) and Löwner's proof for $|a_3|\leq 3$ (1923), culminating in de Branges' general proof. Faheem is continuing his doctoral studies at IISc Bangalore, demonstrating the solid preparation gained through his research on the Bieberbach Conjecture.

Aiswarya M (MSc Colloquium Reading Course)

In this reading course, Aiswarya investigated two key areas: first, the classification of compact surfaces, covering the homeomorphic equivalence of connected compact 2-manifolds to the sphere $\mathbb{S}^2$, a connected sum of $\mathbb{T}^2$, or a connected sum of $\mathbb{RP}^2$. Second, she explored the elementary properties of holomorphic functions on Riemann surfaces and the construction of algebraic functions. Aiswarya is continuing her doctoral studies at TIFR Mumbai, a testament to her strong foundational work in this course.

Vivek Vijayan (MSc Project)

The main goal of this dissertation was to study a generalized notion of convexity, its preservation under biholomorphisms, and its characterizations. The project involved an in-depth study of elementary properties of holomorphic functions on $\mathbb{C}^n$, with particular attention to plurisubharmonicity and its role in pseudoconvexity. Various notions of convexity and pseudoconvexity and their characterizations, including the Cartan-Thullen theorem, were explored.
