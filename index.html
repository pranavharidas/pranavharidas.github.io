Research

My research interests broadly lie in Complex Analysis. Currently, I am studying surface braid groups and, more generally, the mapping class group of hyperbolic surfaces with Chitrabhanu Chaudhuri. I am also exploring the complex analytic properties of Teichmüller spaces.

During my postdoctoral stint, I explored proper holomorphic mappings of a certain class of domains in \( \textbf{C}^n\). In a joint project with Jaikrishnan, we studied proper holomorphic mappings in certain special class of domains in several complex variables (SCV).

During my doctoral studies, I studied quadrature domains in higher dimensions and their properties and certain intersting properties of the Green's function on planar domains.

Lectures on the Geometric Anatomy of Theoretical Physics

Dr. Frederic P Schuller delivered a series of lectures titled "Lectures on the Geometric Anatomy of Theoretical Physics". The lecture series involves building the foundational mathematics needed for theoretical physics (which includes Classical Mechanics, Electromagnetism, Quantum Mechanics, Statistical Physics, Relativity). These lectures start from Logic and pave its way into Bundle Theory eventually. These lectures will be an excellent source to learn the material generally involved in a Topology and Geometry course in Mathematics. A link to the playlist for this course is given here.
